# Discovery Call Workflow

*30-minute framework for qualifying leads and mapping them to the right offer.*

---

## Trigger

- Discovery call booked with a qualified lead
- Run through this framework on every call

## Owner

Whoever booked the call. Both founders attend for larger opportunities.

---

## Pre-flight

Before the call, read:
- `memory/leads.md` — their entry (source, notes, initial conversation)
- `sales/packages.md` — know all offers and pricing
- `sales/positioning.md` — messaging for their audience type
- Research their business (website, LinkedIn, socials) — 10 minutes

Prepare:
- Know their name, company, industry, and how they found us
- Have 2-3 specific questions about their business ready
- Know which package is likely the best fit (but stay open)

---

## The Call (30 minutes)

### 1. Their Situation (5 min)

**Goal:** Understand who they are and what they do.

Questions:
- "Tell me about your business — what do you do, who do you serve?"
- "How big is your team?"
- "What tools and systems are you currently using?"
- "What's working well right now?"

*Listen for: business size, complexity, tech maturity, growth stage.*

### 2. Their Pain (10 min)

**Goal:** Find the real problem — the thing that costs them time, money, or sanity.

Questions:
- "What's taking up most of your time right now that you wish you didn't have to do?"
- "Where are things falling through the cracks?"
- "What have you tried before to fix this?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing in your operations, what would it be?"

*Listen for: manual processes, repetitive tasks, missed follow-ups, scaling bottlenecks, team frustration, data chaos.*

**Dig deeper:**
- "How often does that happen?"
- "What does that cost you — in time, in money, in missed opportunities?"
- "What happens if nothing changes in 6 months?"

### 3. Impact (5 min)

**Goal:** Quantify the cost of the problem and the value of solving it.

Questions:
- "How many hours a week does your team spend on [the pain point]?"
- "What would you do with that time back?"
- "If this was automated and running perfectly, what would that unlock for the business?"

*Do the maths with them:* "If your team spends 20 hours/week on admin at £25/hr, that's £2,000/month. Our system costs a fraction of that and gets better every month."

### 4. Our Solution (5 min)

**Goal:** Map their pain to the right package. Don't oversell — recommend what fits.

Framework:
- "Based on what you've told me, here's what I'd recommend..."
- Walk through the relevant package (from `sales/packages.md`)
- Explain what they get, how it works, and what the outcome looks like
- Use their language — repeat back the problems they described

**Package mapping:**
| Their situation | Recommend |
|----------------|-----------|
| 1-2 specific pain points, small team, cautious | AI OS Essentials |
| Multiple pain points, scaling, wants AI embedded | AI OS Growth |
| Full transformation, strategic, high budget | AI OS Partner |
| Just needs a website | Website package |
| Wants specific automations, not ready for OS | Workflow Build |
| Needs content help | Content Engine add-on |

### 5. Next Steps (5 min)

**Goal:** Get commitment — either a yes or a clear next step.

- State the price clearly: "The setup for this would be [£X] with a monthly fee of [£X]. That covers [summary of what they get]."
- "I'll send you a proposal with everything we've discussed — you'll have it within 48 hours."
- "Any questions before we wrap up?"
- Confirm their email and best way to communicate
- Set a follow-up date: "I'll send the proposal by [day]. Can we book a quick 15-minute call on [day] to go through it?"

---

## After the Call

1. **Log the lead** in `memory/leads.md`:
   - Update status to "Discovery Call Done"
   - Add notes: pain points, budget signals, timeline, which package fits
   - Set next action: "Send proposal by [date]"

2. **Send a follow-up message** within 1 hour:
   ```
   Hi [Name],

   Great speaking with you today. As discussed, I'll put together a proposal
   covering [brief summary of what was discussed] and have it with you by [date].

   If any questions come up in the meantime, just drop me a message.

   Speak soon,
   [Your name]
   ```

3. **Write and send proposal** within 48 hours (see `workflows/generate-proposal.md`)

4. **Follow-up cadence:**
   - Day 3: "Just checking in — did you get a chance to look at the proposal?"
   - Day 7: "Wanted to follow up — happy to jump on a quick call if you have questions"
   - Day 14: "Last check-in — is this still on your radar?"
   - Day 21: Flag as stale, move to nurture

---

## Edge Cases

- **They're not ready / no budget:** "Totally understand. I'll keep you on my radar and share useful content. When the timing's right, we're here." Add to nurture list.
- **They want a discount:** "Our pricing reflects the value and ongoing support. What I can do is [adjust scope to fit budget / start with Essentials and upgrade later]."
- **They want to think about it:** "Of course — I'll send the proposal so you have everything in writing. Is [date] a good time for a quick follow-up call?"
- **They're comparing with competitors:** "Happy to answer any questions. The key difference is [use differentiators from positioning.md]. We run our own business on this system — it's not theoretical."
- **Bigger opportunity than expected:** "This sounds like it could be a Partner-level engagement. Let me scope this properly and come back with a proposal that matches the ambition."
